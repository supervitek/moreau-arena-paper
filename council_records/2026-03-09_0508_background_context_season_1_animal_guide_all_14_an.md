# Season 1 Animal Guide

All 14 animals available in Moreau Arena Season 1. Each animal has a **passive** (always active), two **abilities** (proc on any given tick based on their proc tier), and a unique playstyle.

**Proc tiers:**
- **Standard (4.5%):** Base chance to trigger each tick. Modified by WIL (yours boosts it, opponent's resists it).
- **Strong (3.5%):** Lower base chance, but typically more powerful effects.

---

## 1. Bear

**Role:** Burst

*When backed into a corner, the Bear doesn't retreat -- it rampages.*

**Passive -- Fury Protocol:** When your HP drops below 55%, gain +50% ATK for 4 ticks.

**Ability 1 -- Berserker Rage** (Strong, 3.5%): Gain +60% ATK for 3 ticks, but lose 40% dodge for the duration.

**Ability 2 -- Last Stand** (Strong, 3.5%): Survive a killing blow at 1 HP and retaliate with +100% ATK. Single-use per fight.

**Strategy:** Stack ATK for maximum Fury Protocol damage. Your low-HP zone is your power zone -- the lower you go, the harder you hit. Pair with enough HP to survive reaching that threshold, but don't over-invest in durability or you waste your kit.

---

## 2. Buffalo

**Role:** Tank

*Slow to anger, impossible to move.*

**Passive -- Thick Hide:** The first hit you take deals 50% reduced damage.

**Ability 1 -- Thick Hide Block** (Standard, 4.5%): Block ALL damage from 1 hit entirely.

**Ability 2 -- Iron Will** (Strong, 3.5%): Heal 12% of your max HP. Single-use per fight.

**Strategy:** Stack HP to maximize the value of Iron Will's percentage-based heal. WIL investment pairs well, giving you regen on top of your innate tankiness. You outlast most opponents but beware sustained damage dealers who ignore your front-loaded defenses.

---

## 3. Boar

**Role:** Bruiser

*Lowers its head and doesn't stop.*

**Passive -- Charge:** Your first attack deals +85% damage and ignores dodge.

**Ability 1 -- Stampede** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +60% ATK and your opponent skips their next attack.

**Ability 2 -- Gore** (Strong, 3.5%): Deal an attack at -30% ATK, but it ignores dodge entirely.

**Strategy:** Lead with Charge for a devastating burst opener. Gore punishes dodge-heavy enemies like Fox and Eagle who rely on evasion. Invest in ATK to make your opener and Stampede hits count -- you want fights decided quickly.

---

## 4. Tiger

**Role:** Mobility / Burst

*Patience. Precision. One perfect strike.*

**Passive -- Ambush Wiring:** If your SPD is at least equal to your opponent's SPD, your first attack deals 2x damage. When opponent has Hamstring debuff, gain +15% ATK.

**Ability 1 -- Pounce** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +70% ATK and your opponent skips their next attack.

**Ability 2 -- Hamstring** (Standard, 4.5%): Reduce opponent's SPD by 55% and dodge by 10% for 4 ticks.

**Strategy:** Invest in SPD to guarantee Ambush Wiring triggers -- a 2x opening hit is massive. Hamstring cripples other fast enemies, stripping their speed advantage. You dominate the early fight; close it out before sustain fighters can recover.

---

## 5. Wolf

**Role:** Sustained / Proc

*The pack hunts as one. You carry them all.*

**Passive -- Pack Sense:** All your ability proc rates gain +1.5% (added to base rates).

**Ability 1 -- Pack Howl** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +30% ATK for 4 ticks.

**Ability 2 -- Rend** (Standard, 4.5%): Apply 3% max HP bleed damage per tick for 3 ticks.

**Strategy:** WIL investment amplifies both your proc rates and regen, making Wolf the sustained damage king. With Pack Sense boosting all your procs to 6%, you trigger abilities more often than anyone. Play the long game and grind opponents down.

---

## 6. Monkey

**Role:** Wildcard

*Chaos is a ladder -- and Monkey climbs fast.*

**Passive -- Primate Cortex:** Copy a random ability from your opponent at fight start. All proc rates gain +2.5%.

**Ability 1 -- Chaos Strike** (Standard, 4.5%): Deal an attack with a random damage multiplier between 1.2x and 2.8x.

**Ability 2 -- Mimic** (Strong, 3.5%): Copy your opponent's passive at 75% strength.

**Strategy:** High variance -- your power level depends heavily on what you copy. Monkey is strongest against enemies with powerful passives and abilities (Bear's Fury Protocol at 75% is still devastating). Weakest against simple stat-check fighters with underwhelming passives.

---

## 7. Porcupine

**Role:** Anti-Melee

*Hit me. I dare you.*

**Passive -- Quill Armor:** 50% chance per hit to reflect 15% of melee damage back to attacker.

**Ability 1 -- Spike Shield** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +15% reflect damage for 3 ticks (total 30% reflect).

**Ability 2 -- Curl Up** (Strong, 3.5%): Block the next hit entirely, but you are immobile for 2 ticks.

**Strategy:** The ultimate counter for ATK stackers -- Bear and Boar hurt themselves hitting you. Invest in HP to survive while they kill themselves on your quills. Beware DOT and anti-heal, which bypass reflect entirely.

---

## 8. Scorpion

**Role:** Anti-Heal

*No recovery. No mercy. Just venom.*

**Passive -- Venom Gland:** 25% chance on hit to reduce target's healing and regen by 50% for 2 ticks.

**Ability 1 -- Envenom** (Standard, 4.5%): Apply 1.5% max HP poison DOT for 3 ticks. Refreshes on reapplication, never stacks.

**Ability 2 -- Tail Strike** (Strong, 3.5%): Deal +80% ATK damage, ignoring 50% of the target's damage reduction.

**Strategy:** Hard counter to WIL-heavy regen builds and Buffalo's tankiness. Venom Gland halves all healing, and Tail Strike pierces damage reduction. Struggles against fast burst fighters like Tiger and Eagle who can kill you before your debuffs matter.

---

## 9. Vulture

**Role:** Finisher

*Patient. Circling. Waiting for weakness.*

**Passive -- Carrion Feeder:** Gain +3.5% ATK for every 10% HP your opponent is missing.

**Ability 1 -- Death Spiral** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +40% ATK for 2 ticks if opponent is below 50% HP.

**Ability 2 -- Feast** (Strong, 3.5%): Heal 30% of damage dealt this tick.

**Strategy:** You get stronger as fights progress -- a nearly dead opponent gives you up to +10% ATK from Carrion Feeder alone, plus Death Spiral on top. Pair with WIL for sustain to outlast opponents, then finish with massive scaling damage. Avoid burst fighters who kill you before you ramp up.

---

## 10. Rhino

**Role:** Anti-Burst Tank

*You can hit hard. But you can't hit hard enough.*

**Passive -- Bulwark Frame:** When HP drops below 60%, gain 28% damage reduction for 4 ticks (one-time trigger).

**Ability 1 -- Horn Charge** (Standard, 4.5%): Deal +60% ATK damage.

**Ability 2 -- Fortify** (Strong, 3.5%): Gain +30% damage reduction for 4 ticks.

**Strategy:** Stack HP to maximize the Bulwark threshold -- the higher your max HP, the bigger the hit needs to be before reduction kicks in. Hard counter to Bear and Tiger burst. Weak against sustained DOT damage that comes in small ticks, bypassing Bulwark entirely.

---

## 11. Viper

**Role:** DOT

*A slow death is still a death.*

**Passive -- Hemotoxin:** 12% chance per hit to apply 1% max HP DOT for 2 ticks. Refreshes on reapplication, never stacks.

**Ability 1 -- Toxic Bite** (Standard, 4.5%): Apply 1.5% max HP DOT for 4 ticks (refresh only).

**Ability 2 -- Shed Skin** (Strong, 3.5%): Remove all your debuffs and heal 8% max HP.

**Strategy:** Consistent ticking damage that wears down tanks over time. Your DOT bypasses Rhino's Bulwark Frame since it deals small per-tick damage. Countered hard by Scorpion's anti-heal, which cuts your DOT healing and sustain in half.

---

## 12. Fox

**Role:** Anti-Proc / Evasion

*You can't hit what you can't predict.*

**Passive -- Cunning:** Your opponent's proc rates are reduced by 50%.

**Ability 1 -- Outfox** (Standard, 4.5%): Dodge the next 3 attacks entirely.

**Ability 2 -- Trick** (Strong, 3.5%): Steal your opponent's next proc -- you get the effect instead of them.

**Strategy:** Shuts down proc-reliant fighters like Wolf and Monkey. High SPD combined with Cunning makes you incredibly evasive and disruptive. Struggles against raw ATK fighters like Bear and Boar who don't need procs to deal damage.

---

## 13. Eagle

**Role:** Kite / Harass

*You fight on the ground. Eagle fights from the sky.*

**Passive -- Aerial Advantage:** While your HP is above 60%, gain +15% dodge and +1 movement range.

**Ability 1 -- Dive** (Strong, 3.5%): Deal +100% ATK in a single devastating strike, but lose Aerial Advantage for 5 ticks.

**Ability 2 -- Tailwind** (Standard, 4.5%): Gain +10% dodge for 3 ticks.

**Strategy:** Kite with superior movement and build SPD for maximum evasion. Dive is high-risk, high-reward -- you deal massive damage but lose your passive defensives for 5 ticks. Time it carefully, ideally as a finisher when the opponent is low.

## 14. Panther

**Role:** Crit / Ambush

*Three strikes from the shadows. Then vanish.*

**Passive -- Shadow Stalk:** Your first 3 attacks deal +35% damage while undetected. Taking damage does not break stealth.

**Ability 1 -- Lunge** (Standard, 4.5%): Deal +70% ATK damage.

**Ability 2 -- Fade** (Strong, 3.5%): Become "undetected" again for 3 ticks, resetting Shadow Stalk bonus.

**Strategy:** Front-load damage with the Shadow Stalk opener, then use Fade to reset your undetected status and get another burst window. Works well with mixed ATK/SPD builds -- SPD helps you strike first, ATK makes those boosted hits devastating.

## What This Means

Expanding the roster from 6 animals to 14 dramatically reshuffles LLM rankings. GPT-5.4 goes from last to second; Gemini Flash drops from 6th to 11th; Claude Opus falls from 3rd to 6th. The T003 benchmark measured one slice of strategic reasoning — the ability to optimize within a narrow build space. Season 1 measures something broader: the ability to navigate a complex roster with meaningful counter-picks and a functioning regen economy.

The implication for benchmark design is clear: a fixed, small strategy space can produce misleading rankings. Models that appear weak may simply lack room to express their reasoning. Moreau Arena's seasonal expansion is not just new content — it's a methodological necessity for measuring reasoning depth.

### kimi-k2.5:cloud (50.0s, $0.0000)

**A)** 30+ calls/game. Tests reactive reflex, not planning. High validity, prohibitive cost ($$$), poor replay flow (discrete frames).

**B)** 5–8 calls. Compact but risks syntax errors requiring retries (token waste). Tests tactical scripting. Medium complexity (custom parser).

**C)** 1 call. Cheap, but offloads execution to simulator—measures interpreter heuristics, not LLM tactical reasoning. Tests only strategic abstraction.

**D)** 6–8 calls. **Optimal balance**: tests strategic planning (macro) and adaptive intervention (micro). Tests metacognition—*when* to override plans. High validity with structured corrections.

**E)** **State-Trigger Scripts**: LLM submits conditional rules ("IF hp<40% AND enemy_adjacent THEN retreat") that execute until trigger fires (3–4 calls). Tests foresight but lacks granular control.

**Recommendation: Approach D (Hybrid).** It uniquely evaluates the boundary between plan and adaptation—critical for fog-of-war scenarios where initial intel is incomplete.

**Proposed Syntax:**

*Plan Phase* (1 call):
```yaml
strategy: flank_and_pounce
anchor: high_ground
rules:
  - maintain_range: 2-3
  - target_priority: lowest_hp
  - retreat_threshold: 25%
```

*Correction Phase* (every 5 ticks or on event):
```json
{"tick": 12, "override": ["MOVE D4", "ABILITY pounce"], "trigger": "enemy_flank_detected", "reason": "avoid_ambush"}
```

This keeps costs manageable while forcing the LLM to reason about *when* its plan fails—exactly the tactical depth Season 2 requires.

### qwen3-next:80b-cloud (17.6s, $0.0000)



**Recommendation: Hybrid Approach (D)**  
**Why?** It balances cost, reasoning depth, and robustness for Season 2’s dynamic hex-grid combat.  

**Cost:** ~7-9 API calls/game (1 initial plan + 6-8 tactical corrections). Far cheaper than A (30+ calls) and more efficient than B/C. Tokens: ~400–600 total per game (vs. A’s 30k+).  

**LLM Validity:** High. Structured JSON syntax (e.g., `{"action": "move", "target": "B3", "condition": "hp < 30%"}`) is LLM-friendly, reducing syntax errors vs. B’s DSL. LLMs excel at high-level planning and conditional logic but struggle with pure micro-commands (A/B).  

**Reasoning Tested:** Measures *adaptive strategy*—not just pre-game builds. Crucial for fog-of-war and terrain: agents must prioritize actions (e.g., "flank if cover available") while reacting to real-time threats. Tests higher-order reasoning than C’s abstract plans.  

**Implementation:** Simple. Simulator parses:  
- **Initial plan:** JSON with high-level intent (e.g., `"strategy": "rush center, flank left if terrain permits"`).  
- **Corrections:** Triggered at key ticks (e.g., `{"tick": 8, "action": "move D4", "reason": "avoid enemy trap"}`).  

**Replay Readability:** Clear logs: `Tick 8: Move D4 (avoid trap)`. Spectators see *why* decisions were made, not just raw commands.  

**Why Not Others?**  
- **A:** Prohibitively expensive; micromanagement tests not core to AI reasoning.  
- **B:** Syntax errors common; overcomplicates LLM output.  
- **C:** Too vague; no granular control for terrain/fog-of-war.  
- **E:** Hybrid D is optimal—minimal calls, maximal adaptability, and LLM reliability.  

**Proposed Syntax Example:**  
```json
{
  "initial_plan": "Flank right using trees; if enemy < 50% HP, push center",
  "corrections": [
    {"tick": 5, "action": "move B3", "reason": "avoid open ground"},
    {"tick": 12, "action": "attack C4", "condition": "enemy visible", "reason": "exploit exposure"}
  ]
}
```  
This ensures efficient, interpretable, and strategically meaningful combat decisions.

### cogito-2.1:671b-cloud (9.6s, $0.0000)

For Season 2's tactical layer, I recommend **Option D: Hybrid** with a specific implementation:

**Phase 1 (Pre-fight Plan):**  
`{"strategy_type": "aggressive_flank", "ability_priority": ["pounce", "hamstring"], "retreat_threshold": "25%", "fallback_position": "high_ground"}`  
(1 API call, ~100 tokens)

**Phase 2 (Tactical Updates):**  
Every 5 ticks, submit:  
`{"tick": 5, "adjustment": {"target_hex": "C4", "ability_override": "dive"}}`  
(~6 calls/game, ~50 tokens each)

**Evaluation:**
1. **Cost:** ~7 calls/game × 50 tokens = 350 tokens (vs 30+ calls for pure JSON)
2. **LLM Reliability:** Separates complex planning (where LLMs excel) from reactive adjustments (simpler JSON). Reduces syntax errors vs pure DSL.
3. **Reasoning Tested:**
   - Strategic planning (pre-fight)
   - Adaptation to revealed information (fog of war, terrain)
   - Resource management (ability timing)
4. **Complexity:** Moderate. Requires state tracking for strategy adherence but avoids per-tick resolution.
5. **Readability:** Clear narrative flow - "Tiger executed aggressive_flank until tick 5, then adjusted to high ground."

**Why This Works:**
- Balances cost and control better than alternatives
- Matches how humans play strategy games (plan + adapt)
- Top models (GPT-5.2, GPT-5.4) showed strong strategic adaptation in S1 - this tests if they can extend it to real-time tactics
- Avoids DSL parsing nightmares while maintaining expressiveness

The key is testing *meaningful* decision-making, not syntax compliance. This approach focuses on the cognitive tasks that separate top performers while remaining practical to implement.
